What are
knife patterns?

A knife pattern is a different pattern or drawing on the same skin of a knife. Different patterns have different prices, some patters have a different price gap between them because of the rarity of the pattern.

Why are the patterns
important?

Knife condition aside, patterns really affect the look and the price of the knife, some patterns are more suitable to be used in a game, some patterns are just nice to collect as they are rare. There are multiple patterns for the many knives and skins in CS:GO, and I'll try to sum it up.

A brief overview

Some of the skin patterns I'll be talking about

Doppler is one of the skins with confusing patterns, most of the patterns have similar patterns and can't really be differed between each other. Adding up to the similar pattern, they have a decent price gap, which makes it all more confusing

Fade's are probably the brightest among the knife skins, although CS:GO is all about killing and bloodshed, a rainbow skin seems to fit in quite well. Fade patterns don't have such a wide price gap, they're mostly relatively close to each other, mostly.

Case Hardened skins has many looks, it can be rusty and ugly, gold and shiny or a nice tone of blue. Although there are many variations of Case Hardened skins, they aren't as expensive as the other high tier skins, but if you're lucky enough to get a blue gem (explained later), you'll be swimming in digital money.
