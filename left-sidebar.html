Playside

The playside is the most visible part of the knife, this is the side of the knife you'd have to see throughout the game.

Backside

The backside isn't visible unless you inspect the knife (F), the backside isn't as important as the playside in terms of looks.

The positioning

Some patterns have different positioning, sometimes the best part is at the edge of the knife, which doesn't really make it visible, sometimes this affects the price of the knife.

Knives and guns in CS:GO have 2 view sides, the side of the knife that is mostly visible in game is called the playside, the side which you need to inspect the weapon (F) to see is called the backside. Sometimes knives have different patterns on each side, one side might be blue and the other might be gold. A knife that is visually pleasing is called a 'play-knife', this knife looks nice on both sides, it wouldn't usually have many flaws and you would be happy using it. The other type of knife would be a 'collectors knife', a collectors knife might look really nice on one side, but quite horrid on the other. Collectors knives are usually rare knives in which not many people have, these knives are just nice to have in your inventory.

Centered and Halves

As seen that Slaughter has many solid and visible patterns, these patterns might be shifted and placed at the side, if the pattern is at the side it is usually considered as 'half', half Slaughters might have reduced prices. Those lucky people out there with their slaughter patterns in the middle, this is usually called a 'centered' Slaughter pattern.

Tiger Stripes

It's simple for tiger tooth, the more stripes and the darker they are, the better! Although sometimes there are little/large empty holes between some stripes, its better if all the stripes are placed as symmetrical as possible

Rusty Parts

The positioning on a Case Hardened knife doesn't really affect the price, but if you're looking for a play knife, some of the patterns will make you cringe. Sometimes blue gems have big rusty gaps in them, collectors wouldn't usually care about this as they don't have to look at that rotting part of the knife in game. People looking for a playknife might think twice before buying a blue gem with a rusty chunk in it.
